Our top frequently asked questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How can MiDispensary help me?

We treat patients around the nation with a variety of chronic health concerns. Whether you have previously used or are new to this treatment modality, our team is experienced in helping patients find the most suitable treatment options for their individual health concerns.

How much do consultations cost?

Our Initial Consultations cost $95.00. Follow Up Consultations cost $65.00. Script Rewrites where no appointment is required cost $20.00. Nurse Consultations are complimentary. Medication costs are not included in these fees.

How do I become a patient?

Simply fill in our patient intake and screening forms and book your appointment. If you are not suitable for treatment, you will be given a full refund.

Why choose MiDispensary?

Our qualified healthcare practitioners are endocannabinoid support experts who can help you find the right treatment for your individual needs. Prioritising patient outcomes, compassionate care and alternative options, our Doctors are brand agnostic and prescribe to best suit your specific health requirements.

What pharmacies does MiDispensary work with?

We do not currently dispense medications but we work with a number of pharmacies around the country to help you obtain your prescriptions. You can also take your prescription to a pharmacy of your choice. We will soon be opening a MiPharmacy onsite at our MiDispensary Bondi Junction Clinic.

Disclaimer

MiDispensary is a medical clinic that offers alternative medicine. Alternative medicines can affect each individual differently due to a variety of factors, and as with any medication you may experience side effects. If you do experience any side effects as a result of medication, please contact MiDispensary as soon as possible. For medical advice, always seek the guidance of a trained healthcare practitioner. Alternative medicines in Australia are accessible via the Special Access Scheme or by an Approved Authorised Prescriber and this is regulated by the Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA). You can visit the Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA) website for more information.
